Bea Miles

Bea Miles (1902 - 1973) was one of Sydney's more notable bohemian rebels, and was the inspiration for Kate Grenville's novel Lillian's Story.Bea's eccentricities were well established as a young girl: at the age of 12 she was protesting against the conscription referendum in World War 1, and by the age of 21 The Smith's Weekly newspaper was campaigning for her release from mental captivity at the Gladesville Mental Asylum. Their front page story read 'Mad House Mystery of Beautiful Sydney Girl'.

Later in life she became a bane to taxi drivers from whom she constantly cadged free lifts about town, refusing to pay her fare. She was also well known for her outrageous, disruptive conduct in public places, and her outspoken criticism of political and social authorities.
