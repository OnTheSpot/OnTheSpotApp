The Dirty Half Mile

Kings Cross has long been a place to party, a welcoming home for boozing, whoring and gambling. This strip along Darlinghurst Rd is known to many as the 'Dirty Half Mile' where all the glamour and sleaze of Sydney nightlife is out on proud display.

In the past, cheap bedsits certainly helped infuse the area with a defiantly bohemian culture. But distant wars also scarred its streets with neon.In their book, In the Gutter ... Looking at the Stars: a Literary Adventure Through Kings Cross Louis Nowra and Mandy Sayer write that in the aftermath of the apocalyptic WW1, the strip became "a carnivalesque oasis of randy soldiers, good-time girls, drag parties, boozing, fornicating, and a celebration of the triumph of life over death".

During World War Two the strip took on a brash, neon-lit American hue when new brothels, nightclubs and strip joints mushroomed to feed the demand from cashed-up American GIs desperate for a good night out.But of course it wasn't only the Americans who were dining out - Sydney women were also known to frequent the area, hoping to spend a little time with a fighting-fit young serviceman.
