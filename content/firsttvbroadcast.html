First TV Broadcast

Coca-Cola has been advertising its product from this building at the intersection of Darlinghurst Rd and William St since 1974, making it an iconic landmark today synonymous with the 'Cross.Perhaps less well known is the special status of this location in Australia's television history. It's from here that television was first broadcast in Australia, on November 5, 1956. Well, to be more precise, the first broadcast was from an outside broadcast van parked on Kellett St, right behind the building. A dish placed on the roof of the van beamed the signal back to the ABC headquarters at Gore Hill.And it was not without mishap...ABN-2 crossed to Gore Hill Telecine for the first musical item (a Bach gavotte with the violinist Christian Ferras) which was accidentally transmitted without sound! Then, during the start of the first newsreel the film broke!

At that time part of the building was being used as rehearsal rooms for the ABC's Sydney Symphony Orchestra (SSO).
