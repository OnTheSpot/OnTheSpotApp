The Forbes Club

Whatever the vice - gambling, prostitution, 'sly grog', baccarat, organised crime - 'the Cross has welcomed it with open arms.

In the late 1960s, when thousands of US servicemen were arriving here from Vietnam, six baccarat schools in Kings Cross were converted into a city-wide network of 14 luxury casinos. They operated seven nights a week, all night.One of the more notorious of these was the infamous Forbes Club, located just opposite the ABC's former William St studios at 155 Forbes St.

One Monday night in 1973 on ABC TV's This Day Tonight young journalists Mike Carlton and Stuart Littlemore filmed the Club from the roof of the ABC building. It was a quiet night - a bit early for gambling - and they managed to find little evidence of the Club's illegal activities.

Despite its star-status on prime-time news, the Forbes Club, like all the illegal gambling dens around Sydney at the time, remained open for business.
