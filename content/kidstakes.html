The Kid Stakes

During the 1920s Woolloomooloo provided the location for some of Australia's first feature films.One of these was The Kid Stakes, a 1927 silent film by Tal Ordell based on a cartoon series about a gang of slum kids in Woolloomooloo.In it, the McElhone Stairs set the scene for a particularly poignant moment, in which a posh kid called Albie Snoops stands velvet-clad at the top of McElhone Stairs longing to join the fun down in the Woolloomooloo slums below...

The Kid StakesDirector: Tal Ordell
Released 9 June 1927
