Victoria Heights

Victoria Heights is arguably one of the most controversial property developments Australia has ever seen. Today Victoria Heights dominates the sandstone escarpment separating Woolloomooloo from Potts Point, three towers of 1970s mustard-brick apartments looming large over the narrow, jagged streets of Woolloomooloo below. If you think Victoria Heights dominates the vista today, try imagining the apartments reaching 40 stories high.

That's what the building's developer, Frank Theeman of Victoria Point Pty Ltd, originally had in mind when he bought up a number of the old Victorian terraces along Victoria St in 1971. With the area recently rezoned to allow for higher-density building developments, he planned to bulldoze those 'old, dilapidated houses' to make some fast cash, in a seemingly fail-safe scheme that had the full support of the NSW State Labor Government.

But Victoria Heights was not the easy money-spinner Theeman had hoped for, and instead became one of the most controversial, morally-dubious developments of the time - if not ever - in Australia's relatively brief urban history.
